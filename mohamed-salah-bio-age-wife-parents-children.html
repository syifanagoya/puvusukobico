Mohamed Salah is one of the best players in the world right now. He earned the nickname,” The Egyptian King”, due to his extraordinary ability and performance which has gained a lot of interest in him.

Mohamed Salah biography

Mohamed Salah Hamed Mahrous Ghaly, popularly known as Mo Salah, is a professional Egyptian footballer who plays in the English Premier League.

Mo Salah started his professional career in 2009-2010 when he joined Al Mokawloon Al Arab in the Egyptian Premier League. He spent 3 years in his home club and produced amazingly his stay that paved way for a move to the Swiss Super League. He managed to score 12 goals in his 44 appearances for the Egyptian club.

Salah joined Basel for 2 seasons in the Swiss Super League. After scoring 20 goals in 79 appearances, he was then signed by The Pride of London, Chelsea.
He spent 2 years at Chelsea but he hardly had play time so went out on loan deals. He made 19 appearances and scored only 2 goals for the London club.

He spent a year at Fiorentina as a loan player and later a year at Roma as a loan player before signing for Roma as a full-time player. His 2 years at Roma gave him 83 appearances with 34 goals to his name which helped generate a move back to the English Premier League.

Salah’s return to the English Premier League this time was to Liverpool. After joining Liverpool in 2017-2018, he is still having an amazing time having elevated his team to several glories due to his extraordinary performance. He has so far made 275 appearances and scored 170 goals for Liverpool.

Mohamed Salah career

Mohamed Salah is one of the most priceless gems Africa could ever have. He has raised the banners of Africa so high with his amazing performance.

Since starting his professional footballing career, he has made a total of 526 appearances with a mind-blowing 247 goals. He has won about 9 major trophies including the Swiss Super League, the English Premier League, the FA Cup, UEFA Champions League, UEFA Super Cup, and the FIFA Club World Cup amongst other trophies.

Salah has been with the Egyptian National Team since 2011 and has made a total of 87 appearances for them with 49 goals to his name.

He has also won amazing honors such as BBC African Footballer of the Year, Premier League Golden Boot, Premier League Player of the Season, and FIFA Puskás Award amongst others.

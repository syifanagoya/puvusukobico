What is Ruud Gullit's Net Worth?

Ruud Gullit is a Dutch professional soccer manager and former professional soccer player who has a net worth of $12 million. Ruud Gullit was born in Amsterdam, Netherlands in September 1962. He was a midfielder and forward who spent his youth career with ASV Meerboys and DWS. Gullit made his senior debut for HFC Haarlem and scored 32 goals for the team in 91 appearances from 1979 to 1982. He played for Feyenoord from 1982 to 1985, scoring 30 goals in 85 caps. Gullit scored 46 goals in 68 games for PSV from 1985 to 1987 and 35 more in 117 appearances for Milan from 1987 to 1993. He played for Sampdoria from 1993 to 1994, Milan again in 1994, Sampdoria again from 1994 to 1995, and Chelsea from 1995 to 1998.

Gullit also represented the Netherlands in international competition from 1981 to 1994. He served as a player-manager for Chelsea from 1996 to 1998 and became the manager of Newcastle United from 1998 to 1999. Gullit managed Feyenoord from 2004 to 2005, LA Galaxy from 2007 to 2008, and Terek Grozny in 2011. He captained the Netherlands team that won the UEFA Euro 1988 and set a world record transfer fee when he went from PSV to Milan. He won three Serie A titles as well as two European Cups for Milan. Gullit was named World Soccer Player of the Year in 1987 and 1989 and won the Ballon d'Or in 1987.
